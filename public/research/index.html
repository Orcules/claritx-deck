01 · What we tested, on what data

The signal RSI(14) bearish divergence: price makes a higher high (≥2%) while RSI makes a lower high (≥5 points lower), the two peaks 10–60 trading days apart. Peaks = pivot highs (above the 5 bars on each side).
The data source Financial Modeling Prep (FMP) daily OHLCV, 2005–2026. 509 tickers: S&P 500 + Nasdaq 100 members, SPY/QQQ/DIA/IWM. ~2.6M daily bars stored locally in SQLite. RSI computed in-house with Wilder's exact formula — no API indicator values trusted.
No hindsight A pivot needs 5 future bars to confirm, so entries are taken at the close 5 bars AFTER the second peak — never at the peak itself. All forward returns (5/10/20/60 days) measured from that confirmed entry.
The control group Every event is compared to 20 random entries in the same stock within ±6 months (excluding overlap with the event's own window). This removes the "stocks went up for 20 years" bias — we measure edge, not drift.
Conservative statistics Events overlap and cluster at market tops, so p-values use month-clustered robust errors on paired (event − random) differences; Benjamini-Hochberg FDR across all 87 table cells; non-overlapping cooldown robustness checks.
Hostile audit before trusting anything 20 adversarial AI reviewers hunted the code for lookahead bias, statistical flaws and survivorship issues. 16 confirmed flaws (including a control-group contamination and a volatility-regime lookahead) were fixed and the entire study re-run before any conclusion was drawn.

02 · Finding #1 — the popular belief is wrong, but the signal is real

After a bearish divergence in a rising market the stock does NOT fall — it keeps rising, just more slowly than normal. That relative weakness is real, large-sample, and remarkably consistent:

42.8% vs. random baseline 35.4% (the black marker — large stocks decline in only 35% of random 20-day windows; 50% would be the wrong benchmark)

On average the stock rises -1.4% less than a random entry over the next 20 days — across 25,129 paired events, consistent in 19 of 22 years (one more year, 2013, is a dead tie at −0.002pp, and 2026 is a partial year), p<0.0001 month-clustered, and it survives a 30-day non-overlap cooldown (−1.4%, p<0.0001). The exhaustion-top variant (a stricter 8-condition pattern, 5,300 events) shows the same picture at −1.61%, 21 of 22 years.

08 · The strategy — making money from it consistently

THE CORE — where the money is

Hedged pair in a bear regime

+2.5% per trade · 59% win
  • All 4 entry conditions + short stock / long SPY, equal dollars
  • 1–2% of capital per leg · run 10–15 pairs at once
  • No biotech or binary-event stocks (the verified Moderna lesson: −113% on one short leg)
  • Real bear years produce plenty of setups: 135 in 2008, 124 in 2022 — diversification across them is the source of consistency
SATELLITE — confirmed bears only

Naked short / put options

+2.0% · 58.5% win
  • Only when SPY's own 20-day momentum is also negative
  • Hold 10–20 days · small size
  • Wide +10% stop or none — tight stops destroy the edge (a +4% stop triggers on 57% of trades and cuts the win rate to 37%)
  • Verified warning: without 2008+2018 the edge is just +0.1%. Prefer puts for a capped tail
BULL MARKET — never short

Use the signal to manage longs

-1.3% naked / -1.0% hedged
  • Measured on 20,312 bull-regime events: naked shorting loses -1.3% per 20 days; even the hedged pair loses (-0.4% at 20d, -1.0% at 60d)
  • Instead: take partial profits, tighten stops, delay new buys on stocks flashing divergence
  • That's where the most consistent edge lives: 1.4% underperformance per 20 days, in 20 of 22 years

The three verified prohibitions: ① Never short in a bull market — measured loss even hedged. ② Never use tight stops — small size instead. ③ No biotech / binary-event stocks — the only left tail that truly hurts.
Can the rule be improved with another variable? We tested 34 — honestly, no.

Two full scans hunted for what separates the 297 winning trades from the 206 losers. Round 1: 15 entry-time context variables (RSI levels and drop, price extension, volume ratio, prior run-up, beta, market cap, SPY depth/momentum/volatility, days-in-bear...) plus sector splits. Round 2: 19 indicator and pattern features — MACD, stochastic, the 20/50/200-day moving averages, Bollinger %B and band width, ATR, OBV, relative strength vs SPY, days-since-high, and structure patterns like the inter-peak neckline break. Discipline both times: a discovery pool (bear-market trades outside the rule, n≈1,430) separate from the validation pool (the rule's own 503), train/test thresholds, month-clustered p-values, FDR correction. None of the 34 survived.

Two cautionary tales from the process: "first RSI peak below 70" looked great inside the rule's own trades (67% vs 53% win) — and REVERSED sign in the independent pool. "Stock above its own 200-day MA" was the strongest discovery-pool feature (p=0.008) — and also reversed in validation. Textbook overfitting, twice; without out-of-pool testing both would have been sold as discoveries. Three weak directional threads remain, coherent with each other — the best pair-short looks like a quiet, grinding leader (moderate <16% run-up, p=0.07; low ATR / narrow Bollinger bands, sign-consistent in every slice but never significant; and entering BEFORE the inter-peak low breaks, not after — chasing the breakdown underperformed in both pools). None meets the bar. The rule stands as-is.

The honest translation — what this is and is not

These characteristics predict relative underperformance — and a stock at score 5-6 still RISES in bull markets (its baseline drift is enormous); naked shorting it still loses (−1.4%/20d measured), and even SPY-hedged in bulls loses (−1.5%/60d at top score). Survivorship bias The universe is today's index members, so today's winners are over-represented and absolute returns skew positive. The headline metric — edge vs random entries in the same stocks — is immune by construction.
Costs No commissions, borrow fees or slippage are included. The 60-day hedged edge of +2.5% leaves room for realistic costs; the thinner naked-short edges may not.
