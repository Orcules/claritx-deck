Implementation guide: trading the findings — with leverage that won't kill you

How to turn the validated edges (full research) into executable trades: which instruments, how much leverage the data actually supports, the exact tooling, and a copy-paste operator prompt for the nightly routine. Everything here is anchored to the measured numbers — including the parts that say "don't".

Read this first — the leverage math was measured, and it is humbling. We re-ran the 503-trade daily simulation at increasing leverage. Linear leverage (bigger margin positions) buys return at an accelerating drawdown cost:
Sizing per pairGross exposure capTotal returnMax drawdownWorst year
5% (research baseline)100%+16%−20%−9%
10%100%+23%−22%−13%
10%150%+25%−31%−17%
20%200%+38%−41%−25%

4× the leverage ≈ 2.4× the return for 2× the drawdown — the ratio DEGRADES, because losses cluster (2008, 2020) and one short leg lost −113% (Moderna). The conclusion the data forces: the right way to lever this edge is defined-risk options, not margin. A put spread caps the Moderna scenario at the premium paid. If you insist on linear margin: 10%/pair with a hard 100% gross cap is the best measured risk-adjusted point.

01 · What you are implementing — the three validated edges

Edge A — the bear-regime pair (absolute money-maker) SPY below falling 200-day MA + confirmed divergence + stock within 3% of its 52w high + extension ≥4%. Short stock / long SPY, 60 trading days. Measured: +2.5%/pair, 59% win, 10/11 bear years.
Edge B — the edge-score overlay (relative, all regimes) The 6-condition score (stretch >200MA, RSI1>72, drop>10.6, ext>5.1%, fresh high, low SPY corr). Score 5-6 forecasts −9 to −13pp of 60d underperformance vs the stock's own drift. NOT an absolute short — a ranking and long-management tool.
Edge C — long-book defense (bull markets) In bulls nothing here is shortable (measured: even hedged loses). A high-score divergence on a stock you OWN is the validated signal to harvest: trim, collar, delay adds.

02 · Instruments — ranked by fit to the evidence

RECOMMENDED — Edge APut debit spread on the stock 60-90 DTE (matches the validated horizon). Long ~0.45Δ put, short ~0.20Δ put. Caps the squeeze tail at premium paid; cheapens the high IV you'll face in bear regimes; no borrow fees. Cost: theta + capped upside. Liquidity gate: OI > 500, spread < 10% of mid on both legs.
RECOMMENDED — Edge A coreShort stock + long SPY (classic pair) Exact replication of what was tested. Use only when borrow fee < 2%/yr and the name isn't HTB. 1.5-2% of equity per leg unlevered; 10%/pair max if levering, gross ≤ 100%.
SITUATIONALLong puts (no spread) Cleaner downside but expensive in bear-regime IV; use when IV rank < 50 or when you explicitly want the crash convexity. Risk = premium; size 0.5-1% of equity.
SITUATIONAL — Edge CCollars / covered calls on longs On a score 5-6 divergence in a stock you own: sell 30-45 DTE ~0.25Δ calls (harvest the forecast stall) or collar with the proceeds. The −9..−13pp underperformance forecast is exactly the regime where covered calls outperform holding.
REJECTED BY THE DATANaked margin shorts, 3x sizing The −113% single-trade tail and −41% simulated drawdown at 20%/pair. Also rejected: tight stops (+4% stop cuts win rate 56%→37%), shorting in bull regimes (0/672 rules worked), biotech/binary names.
NOT TESTED — DON'T ASSUMEInverse/levered ETFs, futures baskets The research tested single-stock pairs. SQQQ-style instruments decay and express a different bet (index direction), which the research explicitly does NOT support.

03 · Sizing rules — from the measured distribution

  • Per-position risk: options — premium at risk 0.5-1% of equity; pairs — 1.5-2% per leg (10% max if levered).
  • Portfolio heat: total options premium at risk ≤ 6% of equity; total pair gross ≤ 100% of equity. Hard caps.
  • Concurrency: up to 10-15 positions (bear years produce 124-135 setups; diversification across them IS the edge), max 2 per sector.
  • Why not Kelly: naive Kelly on +2.5%/σ≈12% suggests absurd size — it assumes independent trades. The real series has ~6 effective bear episodes and fat tails; the simulation table above is the honest sizing authority.
  • No stop-losses on stock legs — validated. The exit discipline is time (60d), regime (SPY reclaims 200MA), and profit harvest (spread ≥ 70% of max value).

04 · The stack — tools that do this job

Broker: Interactive Brokers (or any with cheap borrow + good chains) Needed: portfolio margin optional, visible stock borrow rates (IBKR: SLB rates page), 60-90 DTE chains with depth, API if you want to automate (ib_insync).
This repo's scanner — the signal source python run_scanner.py nightly after close (Task Scheduler entry in the README). Outputs ranked setups with edge_score_0_6, 52w distance, divergence stats, regime line. --drop-last-bar if run intraday.
Regime alert TradingView alert: SPY daily close crossing its 200-day SMA (both directions). This single alert arms and disarms the whole system.
Calendar hygiene Check earnings/FDA/event dates inside the 60-day window before entry (broker calendar or earningswhispers). Not part of the backtest — pure tail-avoidance hygiene, the Moderna lesson generalized.

05 · The nightly operator prompt — copy, paste, fill

Paste this into your AI assistant (or follow it yourself) every evening with the scanner output. It encodes every validated rule and refuses the trades the data rejected:

You are my trading operator for the RSI-divergence research system
(research: orcules.github.io/claritx-deck/research). Tonight's inputs:
1) Market regime line from the scanner output: {REGIME}
2) Scanner CSV rows (fresh confirmed setups): {PASTE_SCANNER_ROWS}
3) My current positions: {PASTE_POSITIONS}
4) Account equity: {EQUITY}

Apply these rules EXACTLY — they are validated by 21 years of data; do not improvise:

REGIME GATE
- If regime != "bear" (SPY not below a falling 200-day MA): output NO new short
  entries. Only review my existing longs: for any long showing a fresh divergence
  with edge_score_0_6 >= 5, recommend trim 25-50% / tighten stop / collar.
  STOP HERE.

ENTRY FILTER (bear regime only)
- Keep only rows where: dist_from_52w_high is between 0% and -3%, AND
  divergence price extension >= 4% (high2/high1-1), AND the ticker is NOT
  biotech/pharma and has NO binary event (earnings, FDA, trial, merger vote)
  scheduled within the next 60 trading days - check and tell me what you find.
- Rank survivors by edge_score_0_6 (descending), then confidence.

POSITION CONSTRUCTION (per surviving setup, until limits hit)
- Default: PUT DEBIT SPREAD on the stock, 60-90 DTE: long put at ~0.45 delta,
  short put at ~0.20 delta. Premium at risk = {RISK_PER_TRADE, default 0.75%}
  of equity. Require: option OI > 500 on both legs, bid-ask < 10% of mid.
- If options illiquid: short stock + long SPY equal dollars, 1.5% of equity
  per leg, ONLY if borrow fee < 2%/yr - tell me to check the borrow rate.
- LIMITS: max 10 concurrent positions; total premium at risk <= 6% of equity;
  max 2 positions per GICS sector.

EXITS (check my existing positions every night)
- Time exit: close at 60 trading days from entry.
- Regime exit: if SPY closed back above its 200-day MA - close EVERYTHING, list
  the closing orders first in your output.
- Profit harvest: if a spread reached >= 70% of max value, close it.
- NEVER suggest tightening stops on the stock legs (validated: tight stops
  destroy this edge). Size small instead.

OUTPUT FORMAT
1. Regime verdict + what it permits.
2. Closing orders (if any) with reasons.
3. New entries table: ticker | structure | strikes/DTE | premium | % equity at
   risk | edge_score | the one reason this setup qualifies.
4. Risk dashboard: total at risk, concurrent count, sector counts.
5. Anything you were unsure about - ask, do not assume.

06 · The weekly rhythm

Nightly (2 min)Scanner run → operator prompt → orders for tomorrow's open. Zero new shorts unless regime line says bear.
On regime flip to bearThe hunting season opens: expect a wave of setups within days. Deploy gradually — the best entries cluster over weeks, not one day.
On regime flip to bullClose all pairs/spreads same week (validated exit). Switch to Edge C mode on your long book.
Quarterlyrun_research.py --refresh + confirm_short.py — re-verify the edge still holds on new data before continuing.

07 · Honest expectations

  • This is an episodic alpha sleeve, not a wealth engine: at the recommended sizing it targets roughly +10-25% on deployed capital in a real bear year, near-zero in bull years (capital parked elsewhere), with measured worst-year −9% to −13%.
  • It will NOT beat buy-and-hold SPY over a full cycle (measured: +16% vs +458% standalone at baseline sizing) — its value is making money in exactly the windows when everything else bleeds, and disciplining your longs the rest of the time.
  • Costs not in the backtest: spreads, borrow, theta. The +2.5%/60d gross edge absorbs normal costs; it does not absorb sloppy execution on illiquid options.
